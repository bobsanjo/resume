What's an Info-neer?

Sounds a bit corny, but that's what we call ourselves at Teknowrite. Concatenate Information+Engineer and you get Info-neers. The term has meaning, though. All our info-neers go through a rigorous training on the latest documentation delivery tools, techniques, and best practices. By sharing our knowledge, we are able to deliver what the customer needs.

Clients

Our clients include many of the top technology companies (HP, Yahoo!, Juniper Networks, Adobe) as well as many top Fortune 100 companies.

Yes, I'm an Info-neer

Olivia (Info-neer 1)

We started Teknowrite in the early 90s back when the internet was just taking off. We quickly realized that information and its delivery was going to be a major part of technology.

We called ourselves geeks back then, but that soon morphed into "info-neers." It's sort of a funny-sounding term but we take our roles very seriously. To be an info-neer at Teknowrite, you need at least one technical or science degree. Many of us have two technical degrees, including a number of PhDs.

We specialize in creating long-term partnerships with our clients, helping companies such as HP, Yahoo! and Cisco deliver documentation that goes beyond words on a page or computer screen to create a positive customer experience.
