Technical Documentation Today

I love to write about technology. With two engineering degrees (EE, Biomedical), I have a natural curiosity about technolgy and I love to find the most effective way to communicate it to others.

As Marshall McCluhan said-- the medium is the message. Today, the electronic medium really is part of the message for documentation delivery-- just consider a world without the hyperlink.

Whatever your message, I know how to deliver it in the appropriate medium, format, and style that tells users of your documentation that you are serious about the information you deliver with your product or service.

I consider myself an "Info-neer"-- a person who not only understands the tools for information delivery, but who also has a background and education in engineering and technology, so he can understand the technology and effectively communicate it to the audience.

Today, there's a shift from Framemaker, the industry standard, to XML-based, structured documentation (e.g., DITA). This requires an agile writer who is not only familiar with the XML tools (XMetal) but who is also comfortable with moving documentation to the new format. In other words, the new documentation landscape requires a person who is not afraid of getting their hands dirty with the tools of the trade while at the same time, being able to understand and write about the technology in a timely fashion.
